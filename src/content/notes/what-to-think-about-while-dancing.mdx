<BlockQuote author="Werner Herzog" work="Werner Herzog: A Guide for the Perplexed" medium="book">
When you write a passionate love letter and focus on making sure your longhand is as beautiful as possible, it isn’t going to be much of a love letter. But if you concentrate on the words and emotions, your particular style of longhand–which has nothing to do with the letter per se–will somehow seep in of its own accord. Aesthetics, if they even exist, are to be discovered only once a film has been completed.
</BlockQuote>

If you're dancing, and you're thinking about your steps, or your timing, or the way you look, you're missing the point, which is to embody the music, and to connect with your partner.

Technique matters, but that's what practice is for. The thing to practice while actually dancing is getting lost in it.
