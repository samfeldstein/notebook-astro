Dancing is singing with the body. You don't sing *to* music. You sing a song. Likewise with dance. You don't dance *to* music. You dance music.

---

Is this dance:

<BlockQuote cite="https://www.henrikkarlsson.xyz/p/hacker-mindset" author="Henrik Karlsson" work="How to walk through walls" medium="essay">
Most systems can be viewed at multiple levels. There is a superficial system which pretends to be made of one thing (walls, hens). But actually, it is really made of something else (bits, memory allocations). And if you learn to understand that underlying system, you can find ways to use the lower-level details to steer the system in a way that looks incomprehensible to those who only see the more superficial system.
</BlockQuote>

Or is this dance:

<BlockQuote cite="https://gwern.net/unseeing" author="Gwern Branwen" work="On Seeing Through and Unseeing: The Hacker Mindset" medium="essay">
This is in some sense the opposite of a mathematician: a mathematician tries to ‘see through’ a complex system’s accidental complexity up to a simpler more-abstract more-true version which can be understood and manipulated.
</BlockQuote>

I tend to take a mathematician's approach. I try to cut to the core of [what a thing is](https://samfeldstein.xyz/blog/what-is-rain/). But is that the right approach for dance.

I suppose you need both. You need a forest-level understanding of the dynamic, and you need a tree-level mastery over the individual levers so that you can pull the right one at the right time without hesitation.

To be honest, sometimes these two ways of seeing feel same to me. Maybe it depends on how you define complexity.
