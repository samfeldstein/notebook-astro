<BlockQuote work="Understanding Price Action Trading" author="Bob Volman" medium="book">
The price action trader adheres to a very simple premise: if a high-odds trade cannot be spotted straight off the chart, it is just not there.
</BlockQuote>

<BlockQuote work="Understanding Price Action Trading" author="Bob Volman" medium="book">
The preferable route, by far, is to always put ample time and effort into constructing a personal methodology, suited for all seasons, and devoid of the vagaries and whims of third party discretion.
</BlockQuote>

<BlockQuote work="Understanding Price Action Trading" author="Bob Volman" medium="book">
Regardless of personal preference, all techniques should at least harbor the same objective, which is to exploit repetition.
</BlockQuote>

<BlockQuote work="Trading Price Action Trends" author="Al Brooks" medium="book">
An important point to remember is that the market constantly exhibits inertia and tends to continue to do what is has just been doing. If it is in a trend, most attempts to reverse it will fail. If it is in a trading range, most attempts to break out into a trend will fail.
</BlockQuote>

Institutional investors move markets. Price action trading is about finding their footprints and following in them.

Sideways action happens when supply meets demand. Buyers and sellers both feel that price is fair, so neither wants to push it higher or lower.
