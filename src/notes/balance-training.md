## Core & Strength Training

- **Strengthen Your Core:** Your core (abs, obliques, and back) provides the stable center of gravity needed for one-legged balances and turns. Incorporate planks, side planks, and Russian twists into your routine.
- **Cross-Train with Pilates or Yoga:** These disciplines focus on slow, controlled movements that engage stabilizing muscles often missed in standard dance classes.

## Balance Drills & Techniques

- **Train Your Proprioception:** Your body's sense of its position in space can be sharpened by practicing balances with your eyes closed. Start on a flat foot and work up to a relevé in a parallel passé.
- **Ditch the Barre:** Practice your adagio or basic balances away from the barre early in your practice. If you feel wobbly, use just one finger for light support rather than gripping.
- **Spotting and Focus:** Find a stationary point at eye level to focus on. In turns, your head should be the last thing to leave and the first to arrive back at that spot to prevent dizziness and maintain center.

## Alignment & Movement Habits

- **Check Your Weight Distribution:** Ensure your weight is centered over the ball of your foot rather than sitting back in your heel or leaning too far forward.
- **Slow Down the Movement:** Practice difficult transitions at 10% of their normal speed. This allows you to identify exactly where your alignment breaks or where you lose control.
- **Engage Your Adductors:** Focus on squeezing your inner thighs together during balances to help pull your energy upward and keep your hips square.
