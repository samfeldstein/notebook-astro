Idea from Bruce Lee. Also reflected here: 

<BlockQuote cite="https://gwern.net/unseeing#atoms" author="Gwern Branwen" work="On Seeing Through and Unseeing: The Hacker Mindset" medium="essay">
In stealth games⁠, players learn to unsee levels into patterns of gaps moving around over time—gaps in guard patrols or observability of light/sound—and how to dismantle the level piece by piece until they can go anywhere and do anything.
</BlockQuote>

It's looking past complexity to the fundamental dynamic.

Likewise, photographers try to reduce a scene to an arrangement of lines and shades.

When I dance, I aspire to reduce the music to whatever the musical equivalents of lines and shades are.
